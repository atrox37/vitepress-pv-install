### Step Details

#### 1. Fixed Frame Top and Side Combination

1. Stand up the two fixed frame sides, align them front and back, and fix the spacing with installation limit pieces

<div class="mc-img-row">
  <img src="/images/step/step1/（2）/①.1.png" alt="Step 1-①.1" />
  <!-- <img src="/images/step/step1/（2）/①.2.png" alt="Step 1-①.2" /> -->
</div>

2. Use a forklift to lift the fixed frame top and align it with the installation holes of the fixed frame sides

<!-- <div class="mc-img-row"> -->
  <img src="/images/step/step1/（2）/②.1.jpg" alt="Step 1-②.1" />
  <img src="/images/step/step1/（2）/②.2.png" alt="Step 1-②.2" />
<!-- </div> -->


3. Workers stand on the ladder and insert M16X35 flange bolts through the aligned installation holes of the fixed frame top and sides, then tighten the bolts with an electric wrench (6 places on each side, 12 places total)

This is the long socket of the electric wrench, which replaces the electric wrench assembly.

<!-- <div class="mc-img-row"> -->
  <img src="/images/step/step1/（2）/③.1.png" alt="Step 1-③.1"/>
  <img src="/images/step/step1/（2）/③.2.png" alt="Step 1-③.2" />
<!-- </div> -->

<!-- <div class="mc-img-row"> -->
  <!-- <img src="/images/step/step1/（2）/③.4.png" alt="Step 1-③.4" /> -->
<!-- </div> -->

4. After fixing is complete, remove the installation limit pieces for subsequent use in combination frame limiting

<div class="mc-img-row">
  <img src="/images/step/step1/（2）/④.1.png" alt="Step 1-④.1" />
</div>

5. Place the level on the fixed frame side rail and the fixed frame top beam respectively. If ground undulation causes overall tilt greater than 1 degree, use a wrench to adjust the adjustment nut on the foot to achieve approximate overall level of the fixed frame, otherwise no adjustment is needed
